Mawjat — meaning *waves* in Arabic — is a magazine dedicated to the movements, currents, and waves of creative expression that continuously reshape culture. Founded with a commitment to multilingual cultural journalism, Mawjat provides thoughtful coverage of art, music, film, literature, and performance.

## Our Mission

We believe that culture is a conversation — one that is richer when it happens across languages, borders, and traditions. Mawjat exists to facilitate that conversation by:

- Publishing original criticism and features in Arabic, French, and English
- Spotlighting artists and creators who deserve wider recognition
- Providing rigorous, respectful cultural commentary
- Creating a platform where different cultural perspectives intersect

## Editorial Standards

All content published in Mawjat undergoes editorial review. We are committed to factual accuracy, thoughtful criticism, and respectful engagement with the works and artists we cover. Our editors work closely with contributors to develop pieces that meet our editorial standards.

## Languages & Accessibility

Mawjat is published in three languages because we believe that access to cultural discourse should not be limited by language. Arabic content uses right-to-left text rendering. French and English content is presented in standard left-to-right format. Where possible, key pieces are translated across editions.
